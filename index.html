Introduction

Flock Safety

• Flock Safety is a Software and hardware Surveillance company which began as a startup in 2017 based out of Atlanta, Georgia. They specialize in many different technologies such as drones as first responders, Audio detection devices, and video cameras. Overall, they provide public safety technology for law enforcement, neighborhoods, and businesses.

Flock ALPR Camera

• The technology our research analysis focuses on is Flock Safety's Automated License plate reader (ALPR) Cameras. These cameras are designed to capture vehicle details from stationary, networked cameras to provide information that can be used to assist public safety.

• It works by taking high-resolution images of license plates and vehicle details of passing vehicles, These cameras will then upload this information to a secured cloud platform where they can be viewed analyzed. Flock three main goals are to deter crime, assist law enforcement, and provide communities with data-driven insights about public safety.

Technology Rational

• Our rational behind choosing this technology is because of how flock cameras are said to be providing communities and police departments with fast, data-driven method to investigating and preventing crime. However, this technology has raised social and ethical questions about privacy, data use, and surveillance technology in everyday spaces.

Priceton Manor HOA

• Princeton Manor a HOA located in Knightdale North Carolina installed Flock Safety cameras after a rise of car break in, with the hopes of capturing clear vehicle data than doorbell cameras and provide useful evidence for police. Cameras were place in all entrances and records who leaves and enters.
• The cameras ended up dividing the community, one side feeling that system was invasive while the other side felt supported it for security.​

More Information

Denver City Council

• Denver will stop using Flock cameras after the city council unanimously voted against renewing the contract. Despite their success in reducing car thefts, officials worried about data security and possible access by agencies that work with ICE.
• These worries caused many people to push back over privacy concerns. Denver will finish the pilot program but won’t renew the contract and will create a group to review how this kind of technology should be used.​

Critical Discussion

As show above in Princeton Manor HOA case and Denver's city council decision not renew their contract with flock. We can start to understand how and why flock cameras became socially and ethically problematic.​

Privacy Issues

  • Denver City Council -
    Residents were uncomfortable with police storing large amounts of tracking data.​
  • Princeton Manor HOA -
    Neighbors felt uncomfortable due to their vehicles being captured on flock cameras every time they entered or left the neighborhood.​

Community Disagreement

  • Denver City Council -
    Even though the cameras reduced car thefts, many community members still opposed them because of surveillance fears​.
  • Princeton Manor HOA -
    The community was divided, one side supporting the cameras while other side oppose them.​

Data Use Implications

  • Denver City Council -
    People questioned who controls the data, how long it’s stored, and what it could be used for in the future.
  • Princeton Manor HOA -
    Residents worried that HOA members could misuse the data or track personal patterns, like who visits which home
